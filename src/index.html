28-29th October | VIT UNIVERSITY


DevFest is GDG’s annual Developer festival, celebrating the community and hoping to empower young minds by bringing enthusiastic technocrats and thinkers under one roof. This time in the third iteration, participants get the unique opportunity to learn and interact with industry leaders, learning the ways of the trade from the best.

The event is spread over two days, with a Hackathon lasting 18 hours, and an Ideathon of 8 hours taking place simultaneously.

The first event is the Hackathon, with a goal to create a sprint like event, where participants create, hack, innovate to solve problems while adhering to the spirit of creativity and community that GDG is all about.

The Ideathon on the other hand, presents an amazing opportunity for people who are just diving into the world of technology we all love and cater to. We are bringing in an eight-hour journey where teams collaborate and grow in and around each other, learning the value of teamwork to solve a given problem statement in the most efficient way possible.

Still got questions ?

  • When?
    The event will commence at 7 PM on 28th October.
  • Where?
    Venue details will be up soon, they will also be forwarded to all participants via mail and SMS on their registered mobile numbers.
  • Who can participate?
    Only freshers can participate in the Ideathon. The Hackathon is open to all, however, a participant can only participate in one of those two events.
  • Can I switch between events?
    Yes, if you feel the pace isn’t for you, you can switch either from the Hackathon to the Ideathon or vice-versa up until 12 AM. However, you are allowed to switch only once. You can’t go back after switching, so be careful before deciding.
  • View all
  • What do I need?
    The participants are expected to carry their laptops to the event.
  • Are there any rules?
    Adhere to our Code of Conduct and Rules and Regulations while you participate in the event.
  • Do I need a team?
    Yes, you would be working in a team of minimum 2 members and maximum 3 members. You may come with your friends as a team, or you can team up with other participants at the event. You will however, need to get registered as a team member before the problem statements are handed out.
  • What are problem statements?
    Problem statements are a description of the issues you will be working on. Multiple problem statements will be given across domains, and your team can choose to work on any one of them. Each problem will have 2 parts, a management part and a technical part.
  • I am at the event. What do I do?
    Got your team? Great! Brainstorm and come up with an idea to be presented to our mentor team. They will visit you to hear out your idea and plan of action. they will provide you with suggestions for further course of action. You can brainstorm across multiple domains, but your final idea should correspond to only one domain. Avoid switching ideas because it may lead to you falling behind your peers.
  • I have started working on an idea. Now what?
    Start working on making a model for your idea. You don’t necessarily need to code, but it is accepted nonetheless. Mentors will assist you with your implementation, clarify your doubts as well as point out the potential mistakes you might make. They’re there to help, don’t be shy, ask your doubts away.
  • How do I go about making a model of my idea?

    You would be required to work on building a complete model, including the management and technical aspects of the problem.

    The management part would include :

    • ● the description of your solution
    • ● the resources you would require
    • ● your optimization measures for your business model and your execution method. For starters, methods of optimization would include doing a SWOT or Cost-Benefit analysis of your model, your execution method would consider your market and the demographics of your target audience.

    The technical part would describe :

    • ● approach used to solve the problem
    • ● the technologies you would use in the model
    • ● the methods of their implementation
    • ● alternative measures and technologies available
  • What are the Judging parameters?
    A team comprising of GDG members will evaluate your performance according to the criteria mentioned in the Rules of Ideathon (which will be provided to you) to be announced during the hack, after the pitch(remove this?) and then declare the results. The top teams would be selected to pitch their idea the next evening in front of a panel of judges who will decide the eventual winner.
  • Help !
    If you need any assistance you can write to us at gdgvitvellore@gmail.com or approach any of our coordinators present during the event. All the best, and happy brainstorming!
